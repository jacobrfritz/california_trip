## Day 1: Redwoods and the Bay
To maximize your time, start early just north of San Francisco.
- Morning - Muir Woods: Walk through the ancient groves of Muir Woods National Monument. It provides that essential "giant redwood" experience with well-maintained paths.
- Afternoon - Travel South: Drive across the Golden Gate Bridge and head toward the Santa Cruz Mountains.
- Evening - Santa Cruz: Stay in Santa Cruz. You can walk the Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk for sunset and enjoy a casual dinner on the pier.
Day 2: Big Sur Highlights
This is your primary driving day, covering the most scenic stretch of the California coast.
- Morning - Pfeiffer Big Sur: Stop at Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park. This is where the redwoods grow right alongside the river, offering a cooler, shaded environment before you hit the open coast.
- Afternoon - Coastal Views: Cross the Bixby Bridge and stop at the

McWay Falls overlook.
- Evening - San Luis Obispo: Continue south to stay in San Luis Obispo or Pismo Beach. This gets the bulk of the driving out of the way.
Day 3: The Gateway to Southern California
- Morning - Santa Barbara: Drive through the "American Riviera." Stop at Stearns Wharf or the Santa Barbara County Courthouse for its famous clock tower view.
- Afternoon - Malibu: Wind along the coast through Malibu, stopping at El Matador State Beach to see the dramatic sea caves.
- Evening - Final Destination: Arrive at your chosen beach destination to check in for your final two nights.
Days 4 & 5: Beach Relaxation
For your two days of pure beach time, here are three distinct options depending on the "vibe" you want:
- Option A: Santa Monica & Venice (High Energy)

*   Stay near the Santa Monica Pier. You’ll have miles of wide sandy beaches, the Third Street Promenade for shopping, and the eclectic Venice Beach Boardwalk within walking distance.
- Option B: Laguna Beach (Scenic & Artistic)
- Laguna Beach offers stunning coves like Main Beach and Crystal Cove State Park. It’s perfect if you want beautiful cliffs, tide pools, and upscale art galleries nearby.
- Option C: Huntington Beach (Classic Surf Culture)
- Known as "Surf City USA," Huntington Beach has a huge, flat beach perfect for sunbathing and watching world-class surfers. The Huntington Beach Pier is the heart of the action.
